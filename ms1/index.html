Single-surgeon series (2021–2025) with severe temporal separation: 90% of Versius cases in 2022–2023, 94% of DaVinci in 2024–2025. Only two calendar quarters (2024 Q1–Q2, n=55) had concurrent cases, forming the primary analysis.

Interpretation

No statistically significant differences detected for any outcome. Wide confidence intervals reflect limited effective sample sizes (ESS: DaVinci ~28, Versius ~18). The study was underpowered to detect clinically meaningful differences.

Caution: residual confounding

The global analysis shows apparent Versius advantages for PSM and operative time, but the falsification analysis below reveals residual temporal confounding. These results should be interpreted with extreme caution.

F Falsification (negative-control) analysis

Negative-control outcomes (pT3+, SVI, postop ISUP≥3) are pathology-biology variables not plausibly caused by the robotic platform. Significant platform effects indicate inadequate confounding control.

Key finding

The ISUP ≥3 negative control fails in the global OW analysis (OR=1.89, p=0.025) but passes in the concurrent-quarter analysis (OR=1.06, p=0.92). This confirms residual temporal confounding in the global comparison and validates the concurrent-quarter analysis as the more trustworthy estimand.

Cross-platform skill transfer

Similar breakpoint timing (~11–15 cases) is consistent with transferability of robotic surgical skills. The surgeon began Versius cases after only 13 DaVinci procedures. Versius operative time decreased from median 245 min (first 15 cases) to 155 min (after case 50), approaching the DaVinci mature median of 160 min. No PSM breakpoints were detected for either platform.

B Bottom line
  • The concurrent-quarter comparison (n=55) detected no significant differences between platforms for any perioperative outcome, but was underpowered.
  • The global analysis suggested Versius advantages for PSM and operative time, but falsification testing revealed residual confounding (ISUP ≥3 signal, p=0.025).
  • Learning curves showed similar breakpoint timing for both platforms (~11–15 cases for operative time), consistent with cross-platform skill transfer.
  • The blood loss paradox (unadjusted DaVinci 200 vs Versius 400 mL) was resolved by temporal stratification: both platforms showed 400 mL in concurrent quarters.
  • These findings are consistent with Versius as a feasible platform for RARP, but definitive comparison requires larger multicenter studies with contemporaneous designs.

The primary comparison was severely underpowered (concurrent n=55, ESS ~28/18). IDEAL Stage 2a evidence; definitive conclusions require multicenter Stage 3 studies.
